# Dynamical Stability Analysis

## Overview

An important requirement for any modified cosmological framework is the existence of stable and bounded cosmological evolution across both early- and late-time epochs.

The CoDE-4 framework was intentionally constructed as a weak perturbative deformation of standard cosmological expansion rather than a strongly modified gravitational theory.

As a result, one of the primary theoretical requirements of the framework is preservation of:
- asymptotic stability,
- bounded perturbative evolution,
- stable structure growth,
- and near-standard early-universe behavior.

The current CoDE-4 engine numerically tests multiple stability-sensitive sectors of cosmological evolution.

---

# Bounded Expansion Structure

The CoDE-4 framework modifies the effective vacuum sector through the bounded deformation function:

$$
C(z) = 1 + \epsilon \left( \frac{z}{(1+z)^2} \right) \left( 1 - \frac{0.15}{1+z} \right)
$$

The deformation was intentionally designed such that:
- the correction remains finite,
- the modification becomes asymptotically suppressed,
- and no divergent late-time behavior emerges.

As:

$$
z \to \infty
$$

the framework satisfies:

$$
C(z) \to 1
$$

leading to recovery of near-standard cosmological evolution.

This asymptotic suppression acts as one of the primary stability mechanisms of the framework.

# Perturbative Stability

The perturbation sector of CoDE-4 is evolved numerically through the linear matter growth equation with respect to the scale factor $a$ (where $' \equiv d/da$):

$$
\delta'' + \left( \frac{3}{a} + \frac{H'}{H} \right)\delta' - \frac{3 H_0^2 \Omega_m}{2 a^5 H^2}\delta = 0
$$

using the modified CoDE-4 expansion background.

The resulting perturbation evolution remains:
- finite,
- smooth,
- bounded,
- and non-divergent throughout the tested redshift range.

No runaway perturbative growth behavior currently emerges within the framework.

# ISW Stability Behavior

The Integrated Sachs-Wolfe (ISW) sector provides an important dynamical stability diagnostic because unstable modified cosmologies can produce:
- growing gravitational potentials,
- divergent late-time perturbations,
- or non-physical ISW evolution.

The CoDE-4 engine evaluates the normalized linear gravitational potential evolution on sub-horizon scales through:

$$
\Phi(a) = \frac{\delta(a)}{a}
$$

Current numerical evolution demonstrates:
- negative $d\Phi/d\ln(a)$ across tested redshifts,
- stable potential decay,
- and standard dark-energy-dominated ISW behavior.

This indicates that the framework preserves stable late-time perturbation evolution.
