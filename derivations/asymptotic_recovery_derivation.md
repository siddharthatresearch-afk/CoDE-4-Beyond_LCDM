# Primary Asymptotic Structure

The dominant deformation component satisfies:

$$
\frac{z}{(1+z)^2}
$$

At sufficiently large redshift ($z \to \infty$), the denominator dominates asymptotically:

$$
(1+z)^2 \sim z^2
$$

Therefore, the leading-order behavior isolates down to a decaying coordinate field:

$$
\frac{z}{(1+z)^2} \sim \frac{1}{z} \to 0
$$

This asymptotic suppression forms the primary recovery mechanism of the framework.
